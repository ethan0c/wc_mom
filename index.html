Os Tubarões Azuis · The Blue Sharks

Cape Verde

No country this small had ever played in a World Cup, let alone survived one. Cape Verde did both in the same month.

  • #70FIFA ranking
  • €22MSquad value
  • 525KPopulation

Cape Verde is ten islands in the middle of the Atlantic, home to about as many people as a mid-sized city.

Most of the squad grew up somewhere else — Lisbon, Rotterdam, the suburbs of Paris — sons of a diaspora that never stopped calling itself Cape Verdean.

They arrived as the story everyone patted on the head. They left as the team nobody could score against.

La Albirroja · Garra Guaraní

Paraguay

They gave Germany the ball for two hours, then took the tie away from them in five kicks.

  • #43FIFA ranking
  • €64MSquad value
  • 25%Possession vs GER

Germany arrived with a squad worth more than Paraguay's entire domestic league, and for two hours it looked like it. They had the ball, the chances, the territory.

What Paraguay had was harder to fit in a spreadsheet: a low block that never cracked, a goalkeeper who kept getting bigger, and a country's worth of stubbornness.

A hundred and twenty minutes of no, followed by five answers from the penalty spot.

Les Léopards · Kinshasa rising

DR Congo

They did everything to Portugal except beat them. By the numbers, they did that too.

  • #56FIFA ranking
  • €180MSquad value
  • 52Years since last WC

Here is the strange one. DR Congo out-shot Portugal, out-created them, and spent more of the night in Portugal's box than Portugal did.

By every number the analytics industry has invented, they won the match.

It finished 1–1. If the stats can't even call the games the underdog dominates, what exactly are they predicting?
